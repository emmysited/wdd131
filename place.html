Typical Climate in Uganda

Uganda has a tropical climate with two rainy seasons (March–May and September–November) and two dry seasons. Temperatures are generally warm throughout the year, ranging from 20°C to 30°C depending on altitude.

  • Rainy Season: Heavy rains support agriculture and lush green landscapes.
  • Dry Season: Great for travel and visiting national parks like Murchison Falls and Queen Elizabeth.
  • Humidity: Often high near Lake Victoria, especially in Kampala and Entebbe.

About Uganda

Uganda, often called the "Pearl of Africa," is a landlocked country in East Africa known for its rich biodiversity, cultural diversity, and stunning landscapes. From savannas and forests to lakes and mountains, Uganda is one of the most scenic places on the continent.

Key Highlights

  • Capital City: Kampala
  • Official Language: English and Swahili
  • Population: Over 47 million people
  • Currency: Ugandan Shilling (UGX)

Popular Attractions

  • Lake Victoria: The largest lake in Africa and second largest freshwater lake in the world.
  • Source of the Nile: Jinja is where the Nile River begins its journey to Egypt.
  • National Parks: Bwindi Impenetrable Forest (home to mountain gorillas), Murchison Falls, and Queen Elizabeth National Park.
  • Cultural Sites: Kasubi Tombs, Ndere Cultural Centre, and the Uganda Museum.
